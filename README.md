#  IBM HR Analytics — Employee Attrition Dashboard

# Why I Built This

When I first looked at this dataset, one question 
hit me immediately — why are so many people 
leaving this company?

So I dug in. 1,470 employees. 35 variables.
And what I found was honestly surprising.



##  What the Data Revealed

- **1 in 6 employees left** — 16.12% attrition rate
  (237 out of 1,470 employees)

- **Sales Representatives** had the highest attrition
  at **39.76%** — nearly 4 in 10 people leaving

- **Young employees (18-25)** were leaving at
  **35.77%** — highest of any age group
  (44 out of 123 young employees left)

- Employees doing **OverTime** were **3x more likely**
  to leave — 30.53% vs just 10.44%

- **Low income employees (under $3K/month)** had
  **28.61% attrition** — nearly double the average

- Average salary of employees who left: **$4,787**
  vs those who stayed: **$6,833** — a 30% gap

##  Business Recommendations

1. **Reduce OverTime** — employees doing overtime 
   are 3x more likely to leave

2. **Urgent retention program for Sales Representatives**
   — 39.76% attrition is not sustainable

3. **Salary revision** for employees earning 
   under $3,000/month

4. **Mentorship programs** for young employees 
   (18-25) to improve retention
